# thomasson-kinds-projectibility

**Piece 2 of two** on Amie Thomasson's recent metaphysics, written for a **philosophy** audience.

Working title (provisional): *Function-first without function-in-syntax: projectibility, field-relative kinds, and the limits of Thomasson's deflation*.

## Purpose

A position article (not a review) engaging Thomasson's *Rethinking Metaphysics* (OUP 2025), *Norms and Necessity* (OUP 2020), and *Ontology Made Easy* (OUP 2015). It advances Brett's projectibility-first, field-relative account of kinds as the better vehicle for the function-first turn Thomasson takes.

## Thesis

Three moves. (1) The SFL dependency is dispensable: her deflationary results ride on easy ontology plus framework-neutral functional facts; the function-in-syntax framing does no inferential work it cannot get elsewhere, and she half-concedes this. (2) The opponent she does not meet: she engages natural kinds only on the Kripke-Putnam essentialist axis she wants to deflate; the realist-but-anti-essentialist causal/projectibility tradition (Boyd, Khalidi, Dupre, Magnus) is absent from both books. (3) The constructive alternative: a field-relative, projectibility-first account delivers the function-first payoff while covering the natural kinds she sets aside, and resists her deflation on grounds that share her anti-essentialism.

## The convergence to state honestly

Thomasson's function-first move is, structurally, Brett's field-relativity (a kind earns standing within a field's projective economy; no field-neutral pre-given object). The article should grant this nearly-complete agreement and locate the residual difference precisely: purpose-relativity of projection, the missing-value / is-ought argument, and whether falsifiable perturbation predictions buy more mind-independence than empirical-constraint-within-function.
