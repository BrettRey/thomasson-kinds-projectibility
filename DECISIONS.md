# Decisions -- thomasson-kinds-projectibility

2026-06-29 -- Piece 2 of the Thomasson split is a philosophy position article (not a review), advancing the projectibility-first, field-relative account of kinds. Companion to the linguistics review `../thomasson-sfl-review/`. Sequenced second (Piece 1 is timelier and seeds this).

2026-06-29 -- Frame the relation to Thomasson as near-complete agreement with one located disagreement, not as rivalry. Reason: her function-first move is structurally Brett's field-relativity; overstating the gap would be inaccurate. The residual difference is purpose-relativity of projection and the missing-value argument.

2026-06-29 -- Section 3 spine: she engages kinds mainly on the Kripke-Putnam essentialist axis; Boyd, Khalidi, Dupré, Magnus, and Slater appear zero times across the three Thomasson books searched. Reason: verified by full-text search of the local OCR scans (see `notes/source-verification.md`); confirm against legitimate print indexes before submission.

2026-06-29 -- Drop HPC/mechanism-first framing in favour of projectibility-first throughout. Reason: leading with the maintaining mechanism reverses the order the account intends; projectibility is the criterion, the source of clustering is downstream.

2026-06-29 -- Use CC BY 4.0 for the public repository. Reason: the repository is an academic manuscript repository, and CC BY is the standard permissive license for scholarly prose.

2026-06-29 -- Create a public GitHub repository at `BrettRey/thomasson-kinds-projectibility` and vendor the central house-style/build snapshot. Reason: a public clone should build without relying on symlinks to Brett's local workspace.

2026-06-30 -- Define `function` explicitly as role in a practice, not causal mechanism, biological purpose, or syntactic slot. Reason: the paper needs to keep semantic term-functions, field-inferential category-functions, and syntactic functions at separate explanatory levels.

2026-06-30 -- Avoid `class`, `classify`, and `classification` as technical vocabulary in this paper. Reason: Brett uses those words only for loose grouping, and the argument is clearer with `category`, `sorting`, `profile`, `kind claim`, and `projectibility`.

2026-06-30 -- Treat ordinary linguistic practice as a field, and metaphysics, including hard ontology, as another field. Reason: from a field-relative perspective, ordinary use isn't a neutral court over every specialized practice; hard ontology's defect has to be failure to license projections, explanations, or disciplined contrasts, not mere distance from ordinary talk.

2026-06-30 -- Use the proper-name/proper-noun example as a compact level-discipline illustration. Reason: the same string can have a referential role and a syntactic role without either being read off from the other, which helps explain why function-first metaphysics needn't be function-in-syntax.

2026-06-30 -- Detach the metaphysical argument from SFL for philosophical reasons rather than mainly sociological ones. Reason: SFL may have independent merits, but Thomasson's metaphysical conclusion needs functional pluralism about discourse, not the additional commitments of systemic-functional grammar.

2026-06-30 -- Treat Boyd and Khalidi, not only Dupré, as direct bridges to Thomasson on goal/field-relative kinds. Reason: Boyd's accommodationist field-relativity and Khalidi's causal-network account both allow purpose-relative category choice without making the resulting kinds unreal.

2026-06-30 -- Make demotion discipline the residual disagreement with Thomasson. Reason: her fish and race discussions already allow empirical constraint and failed projection to motivate conceptual change, so the paper's added realist pressure has to be the stronger distinction between retention/usefulness and kind-standing for a specified field and projection.

2026-06-30 -- Use race as the divergence case. Reason: it shows how a category can remain useful for social, legal, public-health, or antidiscrimination purposes while losing biological kind-standing when broad trait projections fail.

2026-06-30 -- Position the Boyd/HPC contrast through verified Lipski, Slater, and Martinez citations. Reason: the paper shouldn't imply that Boyd is merely mechanism-first; the novelty is field-indexed demotion, not projectibility by itself.

2026-06-30 -- Current circulation route is PhilPapers plus direct scholarly send to Thomasson. Reason: Brett specified that route during the submission-checklist review; journal-specific checks are deferred.
