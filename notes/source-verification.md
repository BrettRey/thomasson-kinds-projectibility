## From the close-read of *Norms and Necessity* (subagent, 2026-06-29) -- partially page-checked

These began as a subagent close read. The nomological/scientific-law and natural-kind-generalization claims are now page-checked above; confirm wording for any direct quotation before it enters the draft.

- Modal normativism: metaphysical modal claims express semantic rules; they do not describe or track modal features of reality.
- "Simple realism" but anti-explanatory: modal facts are "downstream" of modal truths, not their ground (the dormitive-virtue argument).
- She brackets nomological/causal/dispositional modality (laws, dispositions, counterfactual support) as a distinct, testable, prediction-bearing tier and concedes the empiricist-realist treatment of it is plausible. (This is the tier a projectibility-first realist occupies; she sets it aside rather than contesting it.)
- Footnote 10 (pp. 122--123): natural-kind concepts are non-arbitrary because they support "predictive and explanatory generalizations" (a projectibility-shaped criterion, cashed pragmatically, with no Goodman/Boyd cited).
- The evolution-doesn't-track-reality passage and the unexplained-coincidence reply: pre-emptive strikes against inferring real joints from a category's usefulness.
