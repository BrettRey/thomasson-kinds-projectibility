# Metaphor Audit

## Current Mix

The draft currently uses several frames at once.

1. **Combat or contest:** `against`, `target`, `defeat`, `displaced`, and the earlier `opponent`/`unmet opponent` language. This makes the dialectic feel like Thomasson is fighting rivals, and the paper is identifying the rival she hasn't beaten.

2. **Courtroom or tribunal:** `neutral court`, `settle`, and `world-answerable`. This works better for the paper because the issue isn't who wins a fight, but what standards have jurisdiction over a claim.

3. **Field or practice:** `field`, `ordinary linguistic practice`, `metaphysics`, `science`, `uptake patterns`, `authorities`, `records`, `defeaters`. This central metaphor should control the paper.

4. **Engineering and testing:** `support grades`, `failure conditions`, `demotion rules`, `tests`, `breakdown`, `intervention outcomes`. This frame is also central: fields license projections, and projections are tested.

5. **Spatial route language:** `bridge`, `route`, `outside`, `redirect`. This frame is mostly harmless, but it can blur the main field/testing frame if overused.

## Preferred Frame

The paper should treat the dispute as a question of **jurisdiction, standards, and tests across fields of inquiry**.

That gives us this stable framing:

- Ordinary linguistic practice isn't the judge of every field. It's one field with its own standards.
- Metaphysics isn't an enemy field. It's another field whose claims need standards appropriate to the work they purport to do.
- Thomasson's essentialist discussion isn't a defeated opponent. It's a covered case.
- Anti-essentialist projectibility realism isn't an enemy. It's an unaddressed realist alternative.
- Projectibility-first realism doesn't defeat Thomasson. It adds a further test discipline once a field, category, and projection are specified.

## Edits To Make

Keep:

- `field`
- `standing`
- `support`
- `failure conditions`
- `demotion rules`
- `tests`
- `world-answerable`
- `ordinary use isn't a field-neutral court`

Replace or reduce:

- `target` -> `focus`, `case`, or `covered case`
- `against Kripke-Putnam essentialism` -> `when addressing Kripke-Putnam essentialism`
- `defeat` when used in dialectical places -> `undermine`, `disconfirm`, or `count against`
- `displaced` -> `supplanted`, `absorbed`, or `made unnecessary`
- `bridge` -> `connection`

Leave technical uses of `defeater` and `defeated` only where they describe projection failure, not dialectical victory.

## Intended Result

The draft should read as follows: Thomasson gives metaphysics a function-first redirection and handles one important essentialist case. The remaining question is whether her framework also absorbs the anti-essentialist projectibility tradition. The paper's answer is no: projectibility supplies a further field-relative test discipline. That's not a battle between realism and pluralism; it's a dispute about what standards a kind claim owes once it's made inside a field.
