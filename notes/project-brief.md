# Project brief -- thomasson-kinds-projectibility (Piece 2, for philosophers)

## Genesis

Companion to the linguistics review `../thomasson-sfl-review/`. Both grew from a reading session on Thomasson's *Rethinking Metaphysics* (2025) and *Norms and Necessity* (2020), read against Brett's projectibility-first, field-relative account of kinds. This is the philosophy-facing position article.

## Audience and genre

Philosophers (easy ontology / conceptual engineering / natural kinds). A position article, not a review. It advances the positive program, using Thomasson as the foil and partial ally.

## Thesis (three moves)

1. **The SFL dependency is dispensable.** Her deflationary results ride on easy ontology plus framework-neutral functional facts; the function-in-syntax framing does no inferential work it cannot get elsewhere, and she half-concedes this (ch 9). (Detail lives in Piece 1; here it is one section, pitched to philosophers.)
2. **The opponent she does not meet.** She engages natural kinds only on the Kripke-Putnam essentialist axis she wants to deflate. The realist-but-anti-essentialist causal/projectibility tradition is absent: Boyd and Khalidi appear zero times in either book (see `source-verification.md`). Her case against realism about kinds engages only half the room, and not the half that shares her anti-essentialism.
3. **The constructive alternative.** A field-relative, projectibility-first account delivers the function-first payoff without the function-in-syntax commitment, extends from her deflated discourses (numbers, properties, modality) to the natural kinds she sets aside, and resists her deflation on grounds that grant her anti-essentialism.

## The convergence, stated honestly

Thomasson's function-first move is, structurally, Brett's field-relativity: a category earns standing within a field's projective economy; there is no field-neutral pre-given object (aggregating across fields is a methodological move, not the discovery of a unity). The article should grant this near-complete agreement rather than manufacture rivalry. Other shared ground: both reject Sider-style structure and the layered picture of reality; both are pluralist; both are anti-essentialist.

## The residual disagreement (locate it precisely)

Not "is projection purpose-relative" (both say yes). The remaining inch: Brett wants the maintaining structure to be objective independent of any field's purpose; Thomasson's missing-value / is-ought point says the world constrains but does not mandate which categories we should use. Brett's strongest counter: his projectibility checks make *falsifiable perturbation predictions*, which are world-answerable in a way "useful for our purposes" is not. The honest question for the article: does that falsifiability buy more mind-independence than her empirical-constraint-within-function already grants? State it as a live question, not a knockdown.

## Framing discipline

- Projectibility-first, not HPC-first. Projectibility is the criterion (Goodman). Whatever sustains the clustering (corrective feedback, frozen accident, network position, isolation) is downstream, not criterial. Do not lead with mechanism.
- Goodman, not Boyd, does the headline work. Boyd/Khalidi enter as the realist tradition Thomasson omits, not as the foundation of the view.

## Proposed structure

1. Introduction: her function-first deflation; thesis that it needs less than it claims and engages only half the kinds debate.
2. The dispensable SFL dependency.
3. The opponent she does not meet (Boyd/Khalidi absent; essentialist axis only).
4. Projectibility-first, field-relative kinds: the alternative; coverage of natural kinds she omits.
5. The residual disagreement: purpose-relativity, missing value, falsifiable perturbation predictions.
6. Conclusion.
